the pulmonary arterioles characterised by progressive in-

Abstract
Objective: The objective of this study was to assess the safe-
ty, tolerability, pharmacokinetics, and pharmacodynamics of
selexipag, an orally available selective prostacyclin receptor
agonist, in development for pulmonary arterial hyperten-
sion in healthy subjects. Methods: This was a double-blind,
placebo-controlled, randomised, multiple-ascending-dose,
up-titration study. Male subjects received increasing oral
doses of selexipag (400–1,800 µg; n = 12) or placebo (n = 4)
twice daily for 3 days each, using incremental steps of 200 µg
between each dose level. Standard safety and tolerability
data were collected. Blood samples were taken to assess the
pharmacokinetics of selexipag and its active metabolite
ACT-333679 and possible effects on platelet aggregation.
Results: Dose levels of selexipag up to 1,600 g were well
tolerated and this dose was identified as the maximum toler-
ated dose. Plasma exposure to ACT-333679 was approxi-
mately 4 times higher than that to selexipag. Steady-state
conditions for both compounds were reached on day 3 of
each dose level, and no accumulation of selexipag or ACT-

The objectives of the present study were to evaluate in
healthy male subjects the safety, tolerability, pharmacoki-
netics, and pharmacodynamics of selexipag administered
according to an up-titration scheme and to determine the
maximum tolerated dose.
Methods
Subjects
Healthy male subjects, aged between 18 and 45 years and with
a BMI between 18 and 28, were recruited in this study. Eligibility
of the study participants was assessed at a screening examination
which took place between 3 and 28 days prior to the first drug ad-
ministration. Due to the potential of selexipag to affect platelet ag-
gregation, subjects were not included if they had a history of a
bleeding disorder (haemostasis, haemorrhagic diathesis, nose or
gingival bleeding, a history of bleeding complications after surgical
procedures such as tooth extraction) and/or an abnormal coagula-
tion, low platelet count or haemoglobin level at screening. Prior to
any study procedure, written informed consent was obtained from
each participant after adequate explanations of the objectives,
methods, and potential hazards of the study. The study was con-
ducted at Clinical Research Services, Mönchengladbach, Germa-
ny. The Ethics Committee of the Ärztekammer Nordrhein, Düs-
seldorf, Germany, and the German Health Authorities (BfArM)
approved the protocol. The study adheres to the Declaration of
Helsinki and was conducted according to good clinical practice.

controlled, parallel-group, up-titration study. A total of 16 subjects
in 2 groups were enrolled with 12 subjects in the active treatment
group and 4 subjects in the placebo treatment group. The starting
dose of selexipag was 400 µg b.i.d. for 3 days. The dose for each
subject was then up-titrated after every third day by 200 g b.i.d.,

Bioanalysis
Concentrations of selexipag and its active metabolite ACT-
333679 in acidified plasma were measured using a validated liquid
chromatography coupled to a tandem mass spectrometry method.
To 50 µl plasma, 100 µl internal standard (deuterated analogues of
the 2 analytes) solution (acetonitrile containing 1% ammonia) was
added. After protein precipitation, the samples were centrifuged,
and 100 µl of the supernatant was transferred to an autosampler
vial. The chromatographic system consisted of a pump, a trapping
column (YMC Co., Ltd., Kyoto, Japan; YMC Hydrosphere C18,
5 µm particle size, 2.1 × 10 mm), and an analytical column (Dr.
Maisch HPLC GmbH, Ammerbuch, Germany; ReproSil-Pur 100
basic C18, 3 µm particle size, 2.0 × 50 mm). Mobile phases to elute
the trapping column consisted of water/acetonitrile (98:
2, vol/vol)
containing 0.1% formic acid and methanol/acetonitrile (50:
50, vol/
vol) containing 1% ammonia, whereas those to elute the analytical
column consisted of water containing 1% ammonia and methanol/
acetonitrile (50:
50, vol/vol) containing 1% ammonia. Mass spec-
trometric detection was performed with a triple quadrupole mass
spectrometer (TSQ Quantum; ThermoFinnigan, San Jose, Calif.,
USA) operating in the electron spray-ionization positive-ion
mode. The samples were quantified using peak area ratios.
The method was linear in the concentration range 0.01 to 10 ng
ml
–1
, and the limit of quantification was 0.01 ng ml
–1
for both an-
alytes. Quality control samples were analysed and their measured
concentrations were used to determine the between-run overall
precision and accuracy of the analyses. Precision (% CV) was
≤ 11.0 and 10.8% for selexipag and ACT-333679, respectively,
whereas accuracy ranged from 98.7 to 103.1% for selexipag and
from 99.3 to 103.3% for ACT-333679.

Safety and Tolerability
All subjects who received selexipag reported AEs. No
serious or severe AEs were reported and no clear dose-
dependent relationship was observed for any type of AE.
However, with increasing doses, the intensity of the
events tended to raise from mild to moderate. The most
frequently reported AEs included headache, myalgia,
nausea, pain in the jaw, arthralgia, skin irritation, diar-
rhoea, pain in the upper abdomen, and dizziness. Head-
ache, myalgia, diarrhoea, skin irritation, pain in the upper
abdomen, and dizziness were reported in both groups,
whereas nausea, pain in the jaw, and arthralgia were only
reported by subjects treated with selexipag ( table1 ). Five
subjects received concomitant medications for the treat-
ment of their AEs. Three subjects at the highest dose of
1,800 µg b.i.d. received paracetamol for their headaches
or myalgias, whereas 2 subjects in the placebo group re-
ceived a combination of anthraquinone glycosides and
salicylic acid (topical treatment) to treat their mouth ul-
cerations. At the end of the study, all AEs had resolved
without sequelae. There were no apparent drug-related
effects on blood pressure, ECG, body temperature, clini-
cal chemistry, and haematology. No cases of orthostatic
hypotension were reported (defined by clinical symp-
toms, e.g., dizziness, combined with a reduction in sys-
tolic and/or diastolic blood pressure from supine to
standing by >20 or >10 mm Hg, respectively). The acti-
vated partial thromboplastin time and international nor-
malised ratio results were normal for all subjects through-
out the study. Selexipag had no relevant effects on the
blood coagulation markers (vWF, sTM, and P-selectin;
data not shown). The 2 cases of prolonged bleeding time
(puncture site haemorrhage) after blood withdrawal ( ta-
ble1 ) were not associated with any change in the mea-
sured coagulation variables. One subject had a positive
faecal occult blood test on 2 occasions, once each at 600
and 1,800 µg b.i.d., which upon a repeat test the next day
was negative. No other signs of bleeding, such as haema-
toma or epistaxis, were reported.

Discussion
This study provides safety, tolerability, pharmacoki-
netic, and pharmacodynamic data for selexipag in healthy
male subjects. The dose was up-titrated starting from 400
g b.i.d., in increments of 200-g steps every third day, up
to a maximum tolerated dose. Using this up-titration
scheme, doses of up to and including 1,600 µg b.i.d. were
well tolerated, and this dose was identified as the maxi-
mum tolerated dose. Of note, single doses of 600 and 800
µg were less well tolerated
[17] showing the importance
of using an up-titration dosing scheme to improve the
tolerability of selexipag in healthy subjects.
Up-titration to an individual patient’s highest toler-
ated dose appears to be the most adequate treatment reg-
imen for IP receptor agonists, including epoprostenol
(i.v.), treprostinil (i.v., s.c., and inhaled) and iloprost (in-
haled). Several studies were performed to optimise and
compare different up-titration schemes for i.v. and in-
haled treprostinil to improve its tolerability
[18, 19] .
Among the reported AEs were several that are related
to the gastrointestinal system, such as nausea, diarrhoea,
(upper) abdominal pain, and dyspepsia. Although diar-
rhoea and (upper) abdominal pain were reported with a
roughly similar frequency in both treatment groups, taking
the low subject numbers into account, nausea and dyspep-
sia were only reported by subjects treated with selexipag.
